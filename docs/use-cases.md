# Cortex - Engineering Use Cases & Workflows

Cortex is built explicitly as the ultimate Engineering OS. It is designed to handle the rigorous demands of STEM fields, including complex math, coding, and heavy technical documentation.

## 1. The Computer Science Student
**Challenge**: Juggling algorithms, code snippets, and theoretical concepts like Big O notation without losing formatting.
**Workflow in Cortex**:
- **Markdown & Code Blocks**: Use the advanced Markdown Editor to write your notes. Paste in Python or C++ code snippets, and they will render perfectly with syntax highlighting.
- **Proactive AI**: Upload your professor's lecture slides (PDF) to the Library. Within 15 seconds, Cortex will automatically generate a Study Guide and 5 flashcards on the topic (e.g., "Dynamic Programming").
- **Export to Anki**: Before midterms, click "Export Anki (CSV)" to download your AI-generated flashcards and import them directly into Anki for spaced repetition.

## 2. The Electrical or Mechanical Engineering Student
**Challenge**: Needing to write complex Calculus/Physics formulas and draw diagrams in the same workspace.
**Workflow in Cortex**:
- **LaTeX Math Support**: Use the Note Editor's native KaTeX support to type equations using `$$`. Perfect for Maxwell's equations or Thermodynamics formulas.
- **Infinite Canvas**: When text isn't enough, switch to a **Canvas Note** (`tldraw`) to quickly sketch out a circuit diagram or free-body diagram using a stylus or mouse.
- **Library Chat**: Upload your dense 800-page Physics textbook PDF. Ask the AI: *"Find the section on Kirchhoff's laws and explain it simply."*

## 3. The AI / Machine Learning Researcher
**Challenge**: Synthesizing information across dozens of academic research papers (e.g., arXiv PDFs) filled with math.
**Workflow in Cortex**:
- **Cross-Referencing**: Upload all relevant ML papers for your research project into the Library.
- **Semantic Search**: Ask the AI: *"What loss functions are used across these three Transformer models?"* Cortex uses its embedding models (BGE) and LLM (Gemini/Llama) to find semantic similarities across multiple PDFs simultaneously and synthesize an answer.
- **Zero Lock-In**: Because your notes are written in pure Markdown, you can click "Export Markdown" at any time to move your research out into Obsidian or VS Code.

---

## 💡 Best Practices

1. **Tag Everything**: Use the tagging system when creating notes. Tagging a note with `#calculus` or `#python` makes finding it during finals week trivial.
2. **Leverage the Background AI**: Don't waste time manually writing summaries. Let the Proactive AI do the heavy lifting immediately after you upload a document, and then tweak the generated Markdown note.
3. **Mix Modalities**: Don't just stick to text. Creating visual memory anchors using the Canvas tool has been scientifically proven to improve long-term retention. Cortex allows you to mix text and visual canvases in the same workspace.
